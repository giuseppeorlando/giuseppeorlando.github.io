IMEX-DG methods & Low Mach Flows

Most of my work builds on a coherent computational framework based on:

  • discontinuous Galerkin (DG) finite element discretizations, offering high-order accuracy, local conservation, and flexibility;
  • Implicit–Explicit (IMEX) Runge-Kutta time integrators, designed to efficiently handle stiff relaxation terms and multiscale dynamics.
Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR) has been employed in conjunction with the DG spatial discretization to locally increase resolution where required.

During my research activity, I have mainly focused on the field of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), spanning a wide range of applications. This work started with my Master’s thesis in 2019, which was devoted to the development of a numerical solver for multicomponent reactive flows, with a particular emphasis on approaches that remain robust in the presence of vanishing species [Orl19].

My first publication in 2022 [Orl+22] concerns the development of an efficient numerical scheme for the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations. The proposed approach combines flexible discontinuous finite element space discretizations with efficient implicit time integration methods and employs an artificial compressibility formulation in order to overcome well-known limitations of projection methods. The solver is included in the official deal.II code gallery (https://dealii.org/developer/doxygen/deal.II/code_gallery_NavierStokes_TRBDF2_DG.html).

Building upon this initial work, a first major methodological contribution is presented in [OBB22]. In this study, IMEX approaches originally developed for ideal gases are extended in a non-trivial way to the compressible Navier-Stokes equations with real gas equations of state, including general cubic equations of state. A tailored fixed point iteration is introduced for the treatment of cubic equations of state, enabling the robust use of experimental thermodynamic data. The resulting IMEX-DG scheme is particulary well-suited for low Mach flows, achieves full second-order accuracy, and is accompanied by an analysis of the employed IMEX-RK method, leading to improved absolute monotonicity properties. Furthermore, refinement indicators specifically designed for non-ideal gases are introduced, allowing for an effective use of AMR in thermodynamically complex regimes.

From a more mathematical perspective, I have analyzed the asymptotic-preserving (AP) properties of a general class of IMEX-RK methods for the Euler equations [OB25]. This analysis allows one to rigorously characterize the behaviour of the schemes in the low Mach number regime and to validate higher-order AP methods, extending those previously introduced in [OBB22]. The analysis is carried out for a general equation of state and considers both single-scale and two-scale asymptotic regimes, with particular attention devoted to the impact of boundary conditions in the asymptotic analysis. A quantitative comparison with other AP time integrators is proposed in [OBR25]., where the stiff dependence of the schemes is carefully analyzed in order to avoid the solution of nonlinear pressure equations for general equations of state.

Geophyisical Flows

My research in geophysical flows focuses on atmospheric dynamics, with applications to numerical weather prediction (NWP) and flows over complex orography, and shallow water equations (SWE). In particular, I develop high-order discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods combined with adaptive mesh refinement (AMR) techniques, ensuring robustness and efficiency in realistic simulations. Notably, for the first time in the literature in this context, I have employed for flows over orography:

  • non-conforming meshes [OBB24a];
  • curved elements [OBB24b],
which have shown to improve the performance of high-fidelity simulations over complex terrains.

For what concerns the SWE, I investigated the interplay between model formulation, topography treatment, and numerical discretization. In particular, the approach proposed in [Arp+25] allows for the use of realistic bathymetric data without requiring any regularity assumptions. These studies naturally extend to other geophysical flow applications, in particular to lava flow modelling [GO25]. In this context, a depth-integrated model is adopted, which can be interpreted as a modification of the classical SWE.

Other

Alongside the core research axes, I developed a filtering monotonization technique [Orl23a] in order to avoid (or at least reduce) the oscillations that arise when high-order discretization methods are used. Its potentialities have been shown in a number of benchmarks, including test cases for the Euler equations of gas dynamics.

More recently, my numerical investigations have also focused on Multi-Resolution (MR) techniques [Orl25+]. within a Finite-Volume context, enabling dynamic adaptation and error control based on wavelet decompositions.

I also investigated for the first time in the literature the use of large language models (LLMs) to assist the construction of finite element solvers for PDEs [Orl23b]. While exploratory in nature, this work opens new directions at the interface between numerical analysis, software design, and artificial intelligence.

Current preliminary research directions include Reduced Order Models (ROMs) and Machine Learning (ML) techniques, with the goal of properly embedding these approaches in full-order discretizations in a hybrid paradigm, so as to enhance efficiency without losing accuracy, in the spirit of Scientific Machine Learning (SciML).
